# Child Protection Policy

## Purpose

The **Front Line Families Support Hub NZ** serves families of preterm twins, including vulnerable infants. This policy outlines our commitments and obligations regarding child protection under New Zealand law.

---

## Legal Framework

This policy operates within:

- **Oranga Tamariki Act 1989** (Children's and Young People's Well-Being Act)
- **Care of Children Act 2004**
- **Family Violence Act 2018**
- **Privacy Act 2020**
- **Health Information Privacy Code 2020**

---

## Core Commitments

### 1. The Safety of Tamariki Comes First

- The wellbeing and safety of children is **paramount** and overrides all other considerations, including data sovereignty and privacy.
- If there is a **reasonable belief** that a child is being harmed, has been harmed, or is at risk of harm, this information **must be reported** regardless of consent settings.

### 2. Mandatory Reporting

Under **Section 15 of the Oranga Tamariki Act 1989**, certain professionals are mandated reporters. While this platform is a digital tool (not a practitioner), we:

- **Never suppress** information that indicates child harm
- **Do not use encryption** to conceal evidence of child abuse or neglect
- **Provide clear guidance** to users about their reporting obligations
- **Display emergency contacts** prominently:
 - **Oranga Tamariki**: 0508 326 459 (0508 FAMILY)
 - **Police**: 111
 - **Healthline**: 0800 611 116
 - **PlunketLine**: 0800 933 922

### 3. Platform Behaviour

The platform will:

- **Not store** disclosures of child harm in the encrypted vault without clear guidance to report
- **Display mandatory reporting information** when keywords or patterns suggesting harm are detected in the journal
- **Never act as a substitute** for professional child protection services
- **Refer users** to appropriate services (Oranga Tamariki, Police, Healthline) when child safety concerns are identified

### 4. AI Agent Guardrails

The AI agent system (Aether Summit and specialist agents) is configured to:

- **Refuse** to advise on concealing information about child harm
- **Immediately surface** child protection resources and reporting pathways when harm indicators are detected
- **Not provide** legal advice on child protection matters - always refer to professionals
- **Log** (in the consent audit trail) when child protection guardrails are triggered, even if the content itself is not stored

---

## What This Means for Users

### For Parents and Whānau

- This platform **supports you** through a difficult journey. It does not monitor, surveil, or judge you.
- If you disclose concerns about your children's safety in the journal or to the AI, the platform will **gently guide you toward support services** - not report automatically.
- **Automated reporting does not occur.** The platform provides information and encouragement, but reporting decisions remain with people (professionals and whānau).

### For Practitioners

- The platform **does not replace** your professional obligations under the Oranga Tamariki Act.
- Your use of the platform does not create additional reporting duties beyond those already required by law.
- The Client-Side Documentor can be used to **record your own professional observations** - these records are encrypted and under your control.
